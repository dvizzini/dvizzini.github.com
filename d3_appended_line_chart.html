Below is a set of autocorrelograms for hourly demand in a service industry, used to investigate inter- and intra-week relations in hourly demand and the suitability of autogressive moving-average models in predicting this demand.

Because autocorrelograms in general, and autocorrelograms of cross-weekly hourly demand in particular, are not familar to most, I integrated d3.js with the jQuery UI package so that each line has an explicit explanation under its tab.

Dotted lines indicate the bounds of approximate 95% confidence intervals.

Click on either a line or tab to learn more about each autocorrelogram.


The above autocorrelations indicate that hour-of-week is essential in predicting demand, but that inter-week correlations of demand during a given hour are not strong enough to be modeled. Knowing that a given hour starts at Tuesday at 7 pm is essential in predicing its demand. Knowing demand for the previous three Tuesdays add little to one's ability to predict.
